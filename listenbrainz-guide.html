We're Adding ListenBrainz Support

Last.fm isn't going anywhere — but we're building a better backup. Here's everything you need to know and how to get set up in 5 minutes.

⚡
Why we're doing this
A lot of you have raised concerns about Last.fm lately — the recent scrobbling changes, how the official account has responded, and a growing sense that the platform doesn't feel like it respects this community the way it used to. We hear you.

So we're adding ListenBrainz as a backup option — a non-profit, open alternative that isn't tied to the same priorities. You're not required to switch, but if you'd rather have your streams recorded somewhere that isn't commercially run, this is for you.
Who built this?

Meet ListenBrainz

ListenBrainz was created by the MetaBrainz Foundation — a non-profit best known for MusicBrainz, the open music database used across the industry. It was built as a free, open-source alternative to scrobbling, designed to never go behind a paywall or shut down. It isn't run by the same team as Last.fm, but it exists because its creators wanted to preserve what Last.fm used to feel like before it became fully commercial.

Setup Guide

Get ListenBrainz in 5 Minutes

Follow the steps for your device. The scrobbler apps below can send plays to both Last.fm and ListenBrainz at the same time — so you don't have to choose.

1
Create your ListenBrainz account All devices
Go to listenbrainz.org → click Sign Up → create your account (it uses MusicBrainz login — same non-profit family).

Then go to Settings → Music Services and grab your User Token — you'll need it for the scrobbler app.
2
Install a scrobbler app iOS
Scrobbles for Last.fm (App Store) — supports both Last.fm + ListenBrainz. Go to Settings → add ListenBrainz with your username + token.

OpenScrobbler — browser-based, works on any device at openscrobbler.com.

Apple Music users on iOS: Use the free Shortcuts automation on the ListenBrainz website → listenbrainz.org/add-data
2
Install a scrobbler app Android
Pano Scrobbler (Play Store, free) — the best Android option. Supports Spotify, YouTube Music, and most streaming apps. Add ListenBrainz in Settings → Services → ListenBrainz → enter your token.

Simple Scrobbler (Play Store, free) — lightweight alternative that also supports both Last.fm + ListenBrainz.
3
Connect Spotify directly Optional — All devices
ListenBrainz has a built-in Spotify import — no separate scrobbler app needed.

Go to listenbrainz.org → Settings → Music Services → Connect Spotify. ListenBrainz will pull your Spotify listening history automatically every few minutes.
4
Add your ListenBrainz username to HopeTracker All devices
Once you're scrobbling to ListenBrainz, go to your HopeTracker Dashboard → Profile page → scroll to the ListenBrainz Preview section → enter your ListenBrainz username → click Check →

You'll see a preview of your streams matched against this week's targets. Your official record is still Last.fm — this is a verification tool for now.

FAQs

Common Questions


Do I have to switch to ListenBrainz?
No. Last.fm is still the official source for your streams and XP. ListenBrainz is being added as an alternative for agents who want it or already use it. Nothing changes for you unless you choose to set it up.
Can I use both Last.fm and ListenBrainz at the same time?
Yes — and this is the recommended setup. Apps like Pano Scrobbler and Scrobbles for Last.fm can send every play to both services simultaneously. You don't lose anything from Last.fm by adding ListenBrainz.
Is ListenBrainz available in India?
Yes, fully. ListenBrainz has no geo-restrictions anywhere in the world. It's run by a non-profit (MetaBrainz Foundation) — not a commercial company — so there are no regional blocks.
Why not Spotify direct integration?
Spotify's API changed in February 2026 — developer apps in "dev mode" are now capped at only 5 users. With 200+ agents, Spotify direct integration is not possible without a commercial approval that they almost certainly won't grant to a fan project. ListenBrainz has no such restrictions.
Will my old stream history transfer?
Your XP and history from Last.fm are already saved in HopeTracker. If you want your full listening history on ListenBrainz too, you can import it from Last.fm at listenbrainz.org/settings → Import.
I set it up — now what?
Go to your Profile page in the dashboard → scroll to the ListenBrainz Preview card → enter your username → hit Check. If the numbers look right, you're all set. We'll let everyone know when ListenBrainz becomes an official sync source.
Ready to set up?
Takes about 5 minutes. Your Last.fm setup is completely unaffected.
